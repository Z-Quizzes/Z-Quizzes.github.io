Bicycle Wheel

Time Left:

1. What does a violent jerk in a wheel system indicate?

  • It indicates the rim is disfigured and each point is not at 90 degrees to the axle axis.
  • The tire pressure is too high.
  • The spokes are too tight.
  • The hub is lubricated improperly.

2. What causes front wheel wobble in a bicycle?

  • It can occur when the front wheel system builds up energy and overcorrects.
  • Low tire pressure alone.
  • Loose handlebars only.
  • Worn brake pads.

3. What is a common cause of back wheel shaking?

  • Loose front fork.
  • Excessive air in the front tire.
  • Bent pedals.
  • It can be caused by worn out rear wheel bearings, an insecure axle, or a buckled wheel rim.

4. What does it mean when the front tire swings slightly to the right and then to the left?

  • The rear wheel is under-inflated.
  • It is a type of wobble caused by instability in the front wheel.
  • It means the tire is fully balanced.
  • The frame is misaligned.

5. What is one effect of brackets in a wheel system?

  • They are used to slow down the vehicle.
  • They determine tire pressure.
  • They change the color of the rim.
  • They influence the stability and alignment of the wheel assembly.

6. How do wheels help in daily life?

  • They allow the rider to roll over the ground with great speed and efficiency.
  • They help in lifting heavy weights vertically.
  • They are mainly for decoration.
  • They store fuel energy.
